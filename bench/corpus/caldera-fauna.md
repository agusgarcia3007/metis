# Field Notes on the Fauna of the Caldera Basin

The Caldera Basin is a fictional highland region studied for its endemic species. These notes
summarize three of its best-documented animals. (This document shares no subject matter with the
Meridian Edge Standard; it is included to test cross-domain retrieval.)

## The Glass Marmot

The glass marmot (*Marmota vitrea*) is a burrowing rodent found only above 2,800 metres elevation.
It hibernates for **eight months** of the year, the longest hibernation of any known mammal. Its
fur appears faintly translucent in direct sunlight, which is the source of its common name. An
adult glass marmot weighs about **4.2 kilograms**.

## The Ashfoot Heron

The ashfoot heron (*Ardea cineripes*) wades the basin's thermal pools. Unlike most herons it is
active at night, hunting the blind cavefish that live in the warm springs. It lays a clutch of
exactly **two eggs** each season. The ashfoot heron is the official emblem of the Caldera
Conservancy.

## The Veil Moth

The veil moth (*Tinea velata*) has a wingspan of just **11 millimetres** and lives for only six
days as an adult. Its larvae feed exclusively on the roots of the basin's sapphire orchid, so the
moth's range is bounded precisely by the orchid's range. The veil moth navigates by polarized
moonlight and becomes disoriented under artificial light.
